Sign in

This page talks to your local server with your bearer token (the same ADAM_TOKEN the Operator Console uses). It is stored on this device only.

Easiest way in: on your computer, start Adam with the Start Adam launcher (scripts\start-adam.ps1) — it opens you already signed in, so you won't see this box. On a phone, open the Operator Console's Connect phone and scan the sign-in QR. To copy the token by hand, run scripts\copy-token.ps1 and paste it above.

This SMS integration is opt-in and stays disabled until you finish setup. It lets you text Adam hands-free (from your phone or Meta glasses); Adam reads the message, acts on it in your vault, and replies as a push notification. Only texts from your own number (OWNER_PHONE) are ever processed — everything else is ignored. This is inbound only: Adam does not send SMS back out.
How it stays private — Adam polls Twilio
Adam does not expose anything to the internet for this. Instead of Twilio pushing texts to a public webhook (which would mean opening a public address), your server quietly checks Twilio every few seconds for new texts to your number, over an outbound, authenticated connection. Nothing inbound is published — this works fine on your private network or Tailscale, exactly like the rest of the app. The only tradeoff is a few seconds of delay between sending a text and Adam acting on it.
Set up inbound SMS

  1. Create a Twilio account and buy a phone number

    Sign up at twilio.com ↗, then buy a number with SMS capability (Phone Numbers → Buy a number). This is the number you will text. Texting it costs whatever Twilio charges you; Adam adds nothing. You do not need to configure the number's webhook — polling replaces it.

  2. Copy your Account SID and Auth Token

    On the Twilio Console dashboard, find Account Info. Copy the Account SID (starts with AC…) and reveal/copy the Auth Token. The Auth Token is a secret — it goes in your .env only, never into a chat, a URL, or a screenshot.

    These are sent only to your own Adam server to run the test below. The token is never saved here, never put in a link, and never logged.

  3. Enter your Twilio number

    The number you bought, in E.164 form (e.g. +15555550123). Adam polls Twilio for inbound texts sent to this number.

  4. Enter YOUR phone number (the owner number)

    This is your own cell number — the phone you'll text Adam from, in E.164 form (e.g. +15555550123). Only texts from this number are ever acted on; everything else is ignored. (From Meta glasses, this is the phone the glasses text through.)

  5. Test the connection

    This makes one authenticated read against Twilio to confirm your Account SID, Auth Token, and number work. It does not send a text, reveal a secret, save anything, or enable the connector.

✓ Verified — turn it on

Your Twilio credentials work. One click and Adam writes its own settings and saves your Twilio values + your phone number for you — no editing files.

Only texts from the phone number you entered are ever acted on. Your Auth Token is saved to your local .env only — never shown, linked, or logged.
