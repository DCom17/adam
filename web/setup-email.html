Email Setup — Adam

Operator Console
Sign in

This page talks to your local server with your bearer token (the same ADAM_TOKEN the Operator Console uses). It is stored on this device only.

Easiest way in: on your computer, start Adam with the Start Adam launcher (scripts\start-adam.ps1) — it opens you already signed in, so you won't see this box. On a phone, open the Operator Console's Connect phone and scan the sign-in QR. To copy the token by hand, run scripts\copy-token.ps1 and paste it above.

This email integration is opt-in and stays disabled until you finish setup. It runs entirely in your own Google account through a small script you deploy — Adam never holds your Google password. It can read your mail and draft replies for your approval. It never sends without your approval, and sending stays off entirely unless you deliberately turn it on. It cannot delete, archive, or trash any mail.
Set up your email bridge

  1. Copy the bridge code

    This is the small script that runs in your Google account. Nothing secret is in it.

  2. Open Google Apps Script

    Open a new Apps Script project ↗

  3. Paste the code

    Click anywhere in the big code area (the file is called Code.gs), press Ctrl+A to select everything, Delete, then Ctrl+V to paste the bridge code you copied.

  4. Save the project — this is what makes the function appear

    Nothing works until you save. In the toolbar just above the code, click the floppy-disk Save icon (💾 Save project) or press Ctrl+S.

    • If it asks you to name the project, type anything (e.g. Adam Email) and save.
    • The function name only shows up in the picker (next step) after you save.

  5. Run setGmailBridgeToken to generate your token

    In the toolbar above the code there's a function picker — a dropdown showing a function name. Click it and choose setGmailBridgeToken, then click the ▶ Run button next to it.

    The first run asks you to authorize your own script to read and draft your mail. Follow these exact clicks:

    • A box says "Authorization required" → click Review permissions.
    • If it asks, pick your Google account. (Often there's no picker — it goes straight to the next screen.)
    • On the "Google hasn't verified this app" screen there's no obvious button — click the small grey Advanced link at the bottom-left.
    • Then click the small grey Go to (your project name) (unsafe) link, also at the bottom-left — it's your own script, so it's safe. (If you never named it, it'll say Untitled project — that's fine.)
    • Scroll down and click Continue at the bottom-right.
    • One more pop-up appears: scroll down and tick the box for "Read, compose, send, and permanently delete all your email from Gmail", then click Continue at the bottom-right again.
    That permission text is Google's blanket Gmail wording — it's the only Gmail scope Google offers, so it always reads that way. Adam does far less than it says. It only reads and writes drafts; it cannot send, archive, trash, or delete anything, and sending stays off entirely unless you deliberately turn it on later (and even then every message waits for your approval). The limits are built into Adam, not into this Google screen.

    When it finishes, the Execution log opens at the bottom with a line like GMAIL_BRIDGE_TOKEN=abc123…. Copy everything after the = sign — that's your bridge token. Paste it into the Bridge token box in step 8 below (you don't edit any file by hand).

  6. Deploy as a Web app

    In the top-right corner there's a blue Deploy button. Click it → New deployment.

    • Next to "Select type" there's a small gear icon (⚙). Click it and choose Web app.
    • Set Execute as: Me and Who has access: Anyone.
    • Click Deploy. If it asks you to authorize access again, go through the same Review permissions → Advanced → Go to (unsafe) → Continue → tick the Gmail box → Continue steps once more.
    If a pop-up shows "This site can't be reached" / "Unable to display page" after you approve — that's fine, it still worked. Just click the red × in the top-right of that pop-up to close it; the deployment finishes and the Web app URL appears underneath.

  7. Copy the Web app URL

    After deploying, Apps Script shows two links. Copy the second one — the Web app URL (it ends in /exec). Use the Copy button next to it.

  8. Paste your bridge URL and token here

    Your token is sent only to your own bridge. When you click Enable email below, Adam saves it to your local .env for you — you never edit a file by hand. It is never put in a link and never logged.

  9. Test the connection

✓ Verified — turn it on

Your bridge is live and your token works. One click and Adam writes its own settings for you — no editing files, no copying JSON.

Email turns on in draft-only mode: Adam can read and draft, but cannot send. Sending stays off until you deliberately turn it on, and even then every message waits for your approval. Your token is saved to your local .env only.
