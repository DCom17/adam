Sign in

This page talks to your local server with your bearer token (the same ADAM_TOKEN the Operator Console uses). It is stored on this device only.

Easiest way in: on your computer, start Adam with the Start Adam launcher (scripts\start-adam.ps1) — it opens you already signed in, so you won't see this box. On a phone, open the Operator Console's Connect phone and scan the sign-in QR. To copy the token by hand, run scripts\copy-token.ps1 and paste it above.

This Voicemail integration is opt-in and stays disabled until you finish setup. When a call to you goes unanswered, your carrier forwards it to a Twilio number; Adam answers in a calm British voice, takes a message, and pushes you the caller's name and a transcript. It's a message-taker, not a chatbot — it never holds a conversation and never acts on anything the caller says. Nothing the caller says is ever run as a command.
How it stays private — Adam polls Twilio
Adam does not expose anything to the internet for this. Twilio answers and records the call on its own; then your server quietly checks Twilio every few seconds for new recordings, over an outbound, authenticated connection. Nothing inbound is published — this works on your private network or Tailscale, exactly like the rest of the app. The only tradeoff is a short delay (usually under a minute, while Twilio transcribes) between the call ending and the message landing on your phone.
Connect your Twilio number

  1. Create a Twilio account and buy a voice-capable phone number

    Sign up at twilio.com ↗, then buy a number with Voice capability (Phone Numbers → Buy a number). This is the number your missed calls will forward to. A toll-free or local number both work. If you've already set up the SMS add-on, you can reuse that same Twilio number — one number does both.

  2. Copy your Account SID and Auth Token

    On the Twilio Console dashboard, find Account Info. Copy the Account SID (starts with AC…) and reveal/copy the Auth Token. The Auth Token is a secret — it goes in your .env only, never into a chat, a URL, or a screenshot.

  3. Enter your Twilio number

    The number you bought, in E.164 form (e.g. +15555550123). Adam polls Twilio for recordings of calls to this number.

  4. Test the connection

    This makes one authenticated read against Twilio to confirm your Account SID, Auth Token, and that the account owns this number. It does not place a call, reveal a secret, save anything, or enable the connector.

✓ Verified — now make your number answer
A live call can't be answered by polling, so Twilio needs a tiny instruction telling it how to greet the caller and record. You paste that once into a free TwiML Bin on Twilio — the same kind of one-time "deploy this" step the Calendar and Email add-ons use.

  1. Personalize the greeting (optional)

    Your first name, so the greeting says "you've reached Diego's assistant". Leave blank for a neutral greeting. The default voice is a deep British voice that needs no extra setup.

    That preview is Adam's own Kokoro voice. Using it on the actual call means hosting the clip somewhere public — optional and advanced. The shipped default (the British voice below) needs no hosting and always works.

  2. Create a TwiML Bin and paste this in

    In the Twilio Console, go to Develop → TwiML Bins (open ↗), click Create new TwiML Bin, give it a name like "Adammail", paste the code below, and Save. Then copy the Bin's URL (shown at the top after saving).

    Click "Build my greeting" above to generate this.

  3. Point your number at the Bin

    Paste the Bin's URL here and Adam will wire your Twilio number's Voice webhook to it for you. (Or do it by hand in the console: Phone Numbers → your number → Voice Configuration → "A call comes in" → TwiML Bin → your Bin.)

  4. Forward your missed calls to the Twilio number

    On your cell phone, turn on conditional call forwarding so calls you don't answer go to your Twilio number. Dial the code for your carrier, then your Twilio number, then call. Common codes:

    Verizon / T-Mobile / most GSM:
   *71 + your Twilio number  → forward when unanswered/busy   (then press call)
AT&T (set each separately):
   *004* + number + #        → forward unanswered/busy/unreachable
To turn it off later:  #21#   (or ##004#  on AT&T)

    Exact codes vary by carrier and plan — if one doesn't take, search "[your carrier] conditional call forwarding code". This is a free carrier setting; Adam isn't involved in it.

Turn it on

One click and Adam writes its own settings and saves your Twilio values for you — no editing files.

Your Auth Token is saved to your local .env only — never shown, linked, or logged. Adam never deletes recordings and never calls anyone back.
