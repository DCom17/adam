What it does

Not a chatbot you ask. An assistant that runs your days.

Most AI answers a question and forgets you the moment the chat ends. Adam holds your whole context and works from it, remembering, planning, and keeping you moving.

A real second brain

Adam keeps a private file system for your life: long-term memory for what's durable, short-term context for what you're on right now. Every project, person, decision, and preference lives in it, so you never re-explain yourself and it never forgets you between chats the way a chatbot does.

Plans your day in a two-minute talk

Tell Adam how your day looks and it builds the plan with you, protecting what matters, adding buffers, catching clashes. Connect your Google Calendar and it puts the day straight onto it and keeps it managed as things move. Planning stops being a chore and becomes a quick conversation.

Checks in, morning and night

Say "good morning" and Adam reads you the shape of your day, assembled overnight. At night it reviews what got done and what slipped; each week it zooms out to the patterns, the wins, and what's drifting. You always know exactly where you stand.

Keeps you organized without effort

Fire a thought at Adam from your phone the moment it lands. It files each one where it belongs (task, plan, memory, project), so nothing gets lost and staying organized costs you nothing. Your head stays clear; the system stays tidy.

Learns how you work best

Adam runs small experiments (quests it sets for you) to find out how you actually operate: when you focus, what drains you, which routines pay off. It A/B tests your own life, keeps what works, and tunes your days around it. It proposes; you approve.

A dashboard for your whole life

Adam runs a personal operating system for you: a live dashboard tracking the skills and attributes you're leveling up, your goals for each day, and how you're really doing across work, health, money, and more. Progress that used to be invisible becomes something you can watch climb, on your desk or your phone.

Built to be yours

Private by architecture, not by promise.

Privacy policies can change. Architecture can't be walked back. Adam is built so there's simply no server of ours in the loop.

How it works

Running in minutes, not evenings.

1
Download the ZIP and right-click → Extract All.
Grab the file named adam-local-… .zip under Assets, not "Source code".
2
Double-click SETUP in the extracted folder. The wizard installs everything for you.
If Windows shows a blue "Windows protected your PC" box, click More info → Run anyway. That's Windows being cautious about new apps; our installer is now code-signed, though a new certificate can still show this until it builds reputation.
3
Sign in to Claude when the wizard asks, or paste your own Anthropic API key. Both are first-class.
4
Open Adam from the desktop icon and start talking.
5
Optional: put it on your phone. The in-app wizard walks you through connecting over your own Tailscale network, and the app installs to your home screen like any other.
Phone & remote access is Adam Plus — free while Adam's in beta.
